Mochi

Mochi is a tradiniol Japanese rice cake made from a type of rice that is pounded into a sticky, chewy texture. It has been enjoyed for centuries and is often eaten during celebratons like the Japanese New Year.

Today, mochi comes in many flavors and styles. Some are filled with sweet red bean paste, while others are wrapped around ice cream. Mochi is loved around the world for its unique texture and delicious taste.

  • Plain mochi
  • Daifuku (mochi with sweet filling)
  • Mochi ice cream
  • Sakura mochi (pink mochi with cherry leaves)
